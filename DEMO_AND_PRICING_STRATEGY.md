## 3. Owner Questions & Smart Answers
*Prepare for these likely questions with professional, high-trust answers.*

* **Q: Is this live now?**
  * *A: "Yes, the preview site is live online at this secure URL so you can test it on your own phone. However, it is running in 'Demo Mode' for review, meaning it is not connected to live restaurant operations or bank payments yet."*

* **Q: Can customers order with it now?**
  * *A: "Yes, customers can place test orders to see how the system works. All orders are marked as 'Demo Orders' for workflow preview. We will open the system for real customer orders once we complete the production configuration."*

* **Q: Can payment be added?**
  * *A: "Yes, absolutely. The system is designed to integrate directly with Paystack, allowing customers to pay securely via card, bank transfer, or USSD during checkout. We will set this up in the production phase."*

* **Q: Can WhatsApp be connected?**
  * *A: "Yes. In the production upgrade, we can set up automated WhatsApp notifications that instantly alert your team when a new order comes in, and send receipt details to customers."*

* **Q: Can staff have their own login?**
  * *A: "Yes, in the production upgrade phase, we will configure a future production login setup. Every staff member, chef, and manager will have their own secure email and password to access their respective dashboards."*

* **: Can the menu be changed later?**
  * *A: "Yes, absolutely. We will build an easy-to-use manager admin panel so you or your manager can add new dishes, change prices, or toggle items out-of-stock instantly."*

* **Q: Can we use our own domain?**
  * *A: "Yes! We will connect it to your custom domain, such as `chikinimonie.com`, so it is fully branded under your name."*

* **Q: Can this work for multiple branches?**
  * *A: "Yes, the system architecture is multi-branch ready. You can easily select which branch (e.g., FUTA South Hub or Agape Junction) receives and processes an order."*

* **Q: What happens after I approve this prototype?**
  * *A: "Once approved, we will collect your final menu details, pricing, and branch hours using our checklist. We will then initiate the production hardening package to secure the dashboards and prepare for public launch."*

* **Q: How long will production take to complete?**
  * *A: "Depending on how quickly we finalize the menu content and select a pricing package, the final production launch on your custom domain will take between 7 to 14 business days."*

## 8. What NOT to Say
*Critical communication boundaries to maintain high trust:*

* **Do NOT say:** *"The system is fully launched and processing real payments."* (Be honest that payment is designed and ready for production setup).
* **Do NOT say:** *"Customers are already actively ordering food through it."* (Acknowledge it is in demo review mode for testing).
* **Do NOT say:** *"Staff logins are production-secure right now."* (Explain that password logins will be built in the production package).
* **Do NOT say:** *"Our delivery system is connected to third-party riders."* (Make it clear that rider routing rules are configured in the next phase).

## 11. Physical Glass Showcase & Counter Workflow (Future Upgrade Option)
*Integrating Chikini Monie's in-store display aesthetic with a digital layer.*

* **The Concept:**
  Chikini Monie operates with a physical food showcase/counter style, where customers can see available meals inside a glass display when they enter. The future production system can match this real-world workflow with a **“Today’s Showcase”** or **“Counter Display System”** feature.

* **Meeting Script to Show the Digital Layer Strategy:**
  > *"Sir, one thing I noticed about Chikini Monie is that the business already works with a physical display/counter style. When customers enter, they can already see many of the available meals inside the glass showcase.
  >
  > So this platform should not just work like a normal restaurant website where every order goes straight to kitchen preparing. The better production version should match how Chikini Monie actually operates.
  >
  > We can add what I call a Today’s Showcase or Counter Display System. This means staff can update what is available today, what is limited, what is sold out, and what is coming later.
  >
  > For example, if Jollof Rice is available, Chicken is limited, or Turkey is sold out, the system can show that clearly. This can help customers understand what is available before ordering, and it can help staff avoid confusion.
  >
  > For the internal side, staff can also have a Counter Order Mode, where they quickly tap the items a customer picks from the showcase, record the order, and mark it as served or completed.
  >
  > So the future version is not just online ordering. It can support your real in-store workflow: food displayed → customer chooses → staff packs or serves → order is recorded → manager sees activity.
  >
  > This is why I see the platform as more than a website. It can become a digital layer around how Chikini Monie already works physically."*

* **Safety Compliance Note:**
  This feature is entirely a future production upgrade. Do not claim it is currently active in the review prototype. Explain it as an optional operational addition to be configured upon upgrading to Option 2 or Option 3.
